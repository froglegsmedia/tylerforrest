Tyler Forrest

Tyler Forrest is a composer, music producer, artist, and filmmaker originally from Abita Springs, Louisiana, now based in Los Angeles. A multi-instrumentalist on guitar, piano, and drums, he spent eight years as part of the house band JamNation, holding down residencies at the Rainbow Bar & Grill and Universal Bar & Grill.

His first feature film score was Search and Destroy, produced with Millennium Films — the start of a scoring career that's since grown to include The Dirty South (score and soundtrack), Herman, Strings, the short film On Her Way Home, and Suicide Disco.

Alongside his film work, Tyler produces and performs across four distinct artist projects — Rowdy Hop, Damn We Dope, Tyler Forrest, and Say Don't Play — spanning country, hip hop, Americana, and pop rock. His music has been placed with MTV, CBS, The CW, Barefoot Wine, Starbucks, and TikTok Shop.

Tyler is also a filmmaker at scaryromantic.com.
